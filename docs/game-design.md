# Game Design Notes

## High Concept

Hockey Smash is a first-person grid adventure about finding hidden fantasy realms inside familiar road-trip places. The player is not a chosen knight. They are a traveler who notices that the world gets weird when you look too closely at exit signs, vending machines, maps, and motel hallways.

## Tone

- Curious
- Slightly eerie
- Funny without being random
- Cozy enough for families
- Strange enough to reward exploration

## Camera And Movement

The player views the world from a first-person perspective. Movement is tile-based:

- Forward
- Turn left
- Turn right
- Optional step backward
- Inspect current tile

The game should feel deliberate, readable, and calm rather than twitchy.

## Core Loop

1. Enter a roadside realm.
2. Explore a compact grid.
3. Read environmental clues.
4. Find items and shortcuts.
5. Resolve encounters.
6. Unlock a route or relic.
7. Return to the road map.

## Encounter Types

Encounters should not only be combat. Early encounter ideas:

- Riddle signposts
- Toll spirits
- Snack-based bartering
- Lost luggage mimics
- Weather puzzles
- Route-planning challenges
- Turn-based creature battles

## Items

Possible item categories:

- Snacks: small healing or trade effects
- Maps: reveal nearby rooms
- Charms: passive bonuses
- Keys: unlock realm gates
- Souvenirs: collectibles and story flavor

## First Prototype Scope

The first prototype should include:

- One realm with a 5x5 or 6x6 grid
- Four-direction movement
- A simple first-person view
- Three inspectable objects
- One locked door
- One item
- One encounter
- A win condition

## Originality Notes

The game may take broad inspiration from classic first-person dungeon crawlers, but should use original setting, art, writing, maps, items, enemies, and interface choices.
